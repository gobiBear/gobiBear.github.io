Introduction¶

The Data Science Pipeline¶

In today's era of information abundance, data is being generated at an unprecedented rate, transforming the way we approach problem-solving and decision-making. Data science allows us to extract valuable insights and make informed decisions from this seemingly endless data. However, the process to doing so is involved and not without risks, as providing innaccurate analysis of data can lead to misiniformation. This project aims to showcase the entire data science pipeline. This can be characterized by four main steps:

  1. Data curation and processing
  2. Exploratory data analysis
  3. Hypothesis testing and machine learning
  4. Interpretation and insights

In this project I will showcase these steps by looking at the United States in terms of racial minority populations and poverty.

Why demographics and poverty?¶

Understanding the demographic composition of different regions is vital for many reasons. Racial demographics influence various aspects of society, including social dynamics, public policies, and economic opportunities. Analyzing this data at a county-level allows us to gain a nuanced perspective on how different racial groups are distributed across the United States, shedding light on regional disparities and potential inequalities. By combining this information with poverty data, we can uncover potential relationships and identify specific racial groups which might be disproportionately affected by poverty.

Step 1: Data curation and processing¶

There are three important datasets used in this project. The first provides information about the population of the United States broken down by age groups, race, and county from 2010 to 2020. The second provides economic information about these counties. Specifically, it provides information about household income and poverty levels. Both of these datasets come from the United States Census Bureau. The United States Census Bureau is a governemnt organization which is responsible for collecting and producing data about the American People and the economy. The third dataset is a JSON file which provides geographic information about the counties. This dataset is from plotly, a python library, and will allow us to create maps with the data.

Data curation¶

Fortunately, all of these datasets come in a form which is directly accessible or downloadable, so we will not need to scrape HTML content in order to get our data.

  • Additionally we will apply a conversion to the dataset to try to make all of the values into numeric data types. This will not work for the string values, but it is important for doing transformations with the demographic values later.

Step 1: Hypothesis testing and machine learning¶

Now that we have done some preliminary data analysis, we can dive a little further in depth. We will create a linear regression model to predict a county's poverty percentage. This model will use the population breakdown of different minorities in a county as the independent variables. First, we use the Statsmodels library to fit an OLS regression to the data in order to conduct a hypothesis test.

The two important characteristic to pay attention to in this summary is the Prob (F-statistic) value as well as the P>|t| column. The probability of the F-statistic allows us to assess the importance of the variables alltogether. Here the null hypothesis would be that the percentage of minorities by race in a county is not correlated with the poverty percentage of that county. Because our F-statistic is essentially 0, using any realistic significance level will tell us to reject the null hypothesis. The P>|t| column tells us essentially the same thing, except instead of being a general measure of significance for all the variables, it gives us a measure of significance for each individual variable. These values are also essentially zero, so for each minority group, we can reject the null hypothesis of that variable not being correllated to the povery percentage in a county. These two characteristics allow us to be comfortable using the regression to make predictions. Another important thing to note is that the R-squared statistic is relatively low at around 40%. This means that only around 40% of variation in the poverty percentage of a county is explained by the minority population and median household income. This will be discussed further in the analysis and conclusion.

Step 4: Interpretation and insights¶

The preliminary analysis, hypothesis testing, and machine learning leave us with some takeaways. Firstly, from the preliminary analysis, we saw that the distribution of minorities in the United States is NOT even. That is to say, there are places with far more minorities both by total number and more importantly by percentage. Secondly, we can say the same about poverty in the United States. It is not evenly distributed. There appears to be at least some wealth inequality at least puerly by geographical location.
From the hypothesis testing and machine learning we can better connect these two ideas. Our linear model confirmed that there is a statistically significant relationship between a county's demographics and the poverty rate in that county. This is not to say that a county's poverty rate is solely based on the demographic. Rather, from our R squared statistic we can say that a county's poverty rate is around 40% based on the demographic breakdown. While this is not the whole story, it is important to think about why this part of the story can account for so much of the poverty rate.
The United States is built upon racial inequality and social injustice. This analysis shows to us that these inequalities are still prevelant at least in 2020, and likely today as well. Analyzing the data in order to tease out these trends which demonstrate racial inequality is important for two reasons. It is important because there are still people who deny the existence of these systemic inequalities. In spite of all of the misinformation and fearmongering, it is important to continue to strive to produce accurate and significant statistical analysis using data science. Additionally, it is important because a continuing effor to analyze this data allows policy makers to be adaptive in their efforts to help alleave the inequalities.
